## What Types of Coverage Can You Get Quotes On?

Not sure which type of life insurance is right for you? Here&apos;s a quick breakdown of the options we can help you explore:

### Term Life Insurance

**Best for:** Families who want affordable coverage for a specific period (10, 20, or 30 years)

[Term life insurance](/term-life-insurance) is the most straightforward option. You pay a monthly premium, and if something happens to you during the term, your beneficiaries receive the death benefit. It&apos;s simple, budget-friendly, and perfect for covering things like a mortgage, college tuition, or income replacement.

### Whole Life Insurance

**Best for:** People who want lifelong coverage and a cash value component

Whole life insurance never expires as long as you pay your premiums. It also builds cash value over time, which you can borrow against if needed. Premiums are higher than term, but the coverage is permanent.

### Final Expense Insurance

**Best for:** Covering end-of-life costs like funeral expenses, medical bills, or small debts

[Final expense insurance](/final-expense-insurance) (sometimes called burial insurance) typically offers smaller coverage amounts: usually between **$5,000 and $25,000**. It&apos;s designed to make sure your loved ones aren&apos;t stuck with unexpected costs during an already difficult time.

Many final expense policies are **guaranteed issue**, meaning you can qualify even with health conditions. Learn more in our guide to [guaranteed issue life insurance](/blog/guaranteed-issue-life-insurance-explained).

## &quot;How Much Life Insurance Do I Need?&quot;

This is one of the most common questions we hear: and the answer depends on your unique situation.

A general rule of thumb is **10-12 times your annual income**, but that&apos;s just a starting point. You&apos;ll also want to consider:

- Outstanding debts (mortgage, car loans, credit cards)
- Future expenses (college tuition, childcare)
- Your spouse&apos;s income and financial needs
- Final expenses and emergency funds

During your quote call, we can help you run through these numbers so you feel confident in your coverage amount.
